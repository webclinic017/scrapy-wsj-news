This was to offset the biggest short-term hit to aggregate demand in U.S. history. The consumer-price-index report reveals that big relative price shifts, notably for energy, food and vehicles, and base effects lie behind the rise in inflation. These are largely self-correcting in a matter of months, not years. Inflation worrywarts can sleep easy: If high inflation threatens to become persistent because the public’s inflation expectations shoot up, the Fed will counter with monetary tightening. Thanks to years of monetary easing, it has oodles of ammunition at the ready.
