His troop deployments near Ukraine’s border were failing, and new popular uprisings could topple autocrats in other former Soviet republics, complicating the Russian president’s strategic calculations.
The unspoken corollary to this theorizing is that the last thing America or Europe should do is take provocative action regarding Ukraine or other countries caught in the gray zone between the North Atlantic Treaty Organization’s eastern borders and Russia’s western ones. Kazakhstan is a Kremlin problem where “America has no real leverage,” as Politico put this instant conventional wisdom. Predictably, the State Department’s first reaction to the Kazakh crisis was moral equivalency, calling “for restraint by both the authorities and protestors.”

2022-01-09 12:27:00 Opinion Safe Travels and the 5G Service Launch Regulators do well to protect pilots and passengers from interference. Transportation Secretary Pete Buttigieg and Federal Aviation Administrator Stephen Dickson are rightly defending U.S. leadership in aviation safety—and protecting passengers and shippers—by insisting that telecom companies ensure safety before launching new 5G service that could damage pilots’ ability to navigate airliners (“Buttigieg’s FAA and 5G Mid-Air Collision,” Review & Outlook, Jan. 4).
New AT&T and Verizon service could create interference known to cause the radio altimeters—which tell pilots how far planes are above the ground—to provide faulty data or even stop working. For years, pilots urged the wireless industry to collaborate to ensure safety, but until the Transportation Department and FAA intervened, the industry dismissed our concerns.
