One of the new Olympic sports making its debut in Tokyo is sport climbing, which enters competition on Aug. 3. It consists of three disciplines, all of which the athletes must master in the combined format. In lead climbing, competitors climb a wall as high as they can manage within six minutes while secured to safety ropes. The third discipline to test the climbers is known as “bouldering.” In this event, climbers must complete as many fixed routes as possible within four minutes, on a wall that is about 15 feet high with an array of different challenges. “Bouldering” might seem like an unusual term for an athletic endeavor carried out on an artificial rock wall, with no actual boulders in sight. “Boulder” itself is a word that likely derives from a Scandinavian source, related to the Swedish term “bullersten.” A “bullersten” is a large stone found in a stream, and it can be translated as either “rounded stone” or “roaring stone”—the “roaring” referring to water noisily rushing past it.
